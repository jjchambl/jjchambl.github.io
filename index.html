About Me

I love helping companies find their image and crafting a team of dedicated, hard-working professionals. At Soccer Shots, I taught soccer to mainly preschool-aged children in the Birmingham area; the internet made the proliferation of ideas and information possible on a much larger scale than ever before possible for this wonderful sport, and social media continues to drive a significant portion of its growth. My work took advantage of this growth by cultivating an online community of parents through an online client registration system, monthly newsletter, and social media campaigns.

During my four years with Soccer Shots, our registrations increased from about 450 in January of 2012 to over 1,000 children in December of 2015. My work experience encompasses marketing (both digital and more traditional forms), education, coaching, human resources, theology, and historical research. My recent coding projects include reconstructing the CoverSharks website using PlatypusTS, the Bham Brews project using Angular.js, and the Loan Ranger app using Polymer. Links to my deployed websites are located in the Projects section of this page, and the code is located in my github account linked at the bottom; some of my written work is also linked below.

Depot/U Capstone Project (Expected: April 29,2016)

The Depot/U Capstone Project is scheduled for presentation on April 29,2016. Using the Angular.js framework, our group will highlight and interact with APIs from Birmingham's four craft breweries, offering information, social interactivity, and links to professional associations pertinent to the craft beer industry. Pending its acceptance as a successful capstone project, we will pursue deployment of this website as a Cordova app.

Magic City Coders Project (Expected: May 13, 2016

"Magic City Coders" is the moniker given to the second inaugural collaboration between BBVA Compass, S|ngular, UAB, and Innovation Depot. The program selects a total of eight graduates drawn from UAB's Computer Science Department and Innovation Depot's Depot/U Front End Engineering program. In partnership with senior developers at S|ngular and BBVA, teams of two students each will spend two weeks innovating and developing software that is disruptive to the banking industry. This two-week intensive internship will utilize the Polymer framework, and will utilize a SCRUM methodology. Presentation of student projects will be done the week of August 22-28 during Birmingham's Innovation Week.
